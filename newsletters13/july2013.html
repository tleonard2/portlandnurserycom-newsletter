Easy ideas for entertaining

Oregon summers are the perfect time for entertaining outdoors. Gardens can be a beautiful setting for events as large as a wedding or small as an impromptu brunch. Here are a few quick ideas to help spruce up the garden and decorate for any type of event.

ADD COLOR
Try “drop-in gardening” for instant color. The nursery is full of colorful annuals and perennials in larger pots which can be quickly tucked into empty spots (pot and all) to spruce up before company arrives.

Before After

FLUFF AND TIDY
Pruning the flopping foliage and suckering sprouts helps tidy the garden and provide interesting material for floral arrangements.

It is common for a few stray stalks of Crocosmia ‘Lucifer’ to escape your staking system. We used the leaves to wrap a tall vase to add some interest. The bright red suckers of Crape Myrtle make lovely filler for a small bouquet.

LIGHT IT UP!
Orchid pots can make perfect lanterns on tables or placed strategically through out the garden. Or create a chandelier with Christmas lights, garden ornaments, and objects around the house.

TABLE ROOM
Small end tables or pedestals can easily be created with ceramic or terra cotta pots and saucers. A brightly glazed pot can add a splash of color too.

CURTAIN CALL
Curtains or spare pieces of fabric can be hung from trees or other areas to make a private alcove or create a cool shady spot.

STAY COOL
Line an unused ceramic pots or wooden planters with clear garbage bags to create a decorative ice bucket.

CATEGORIES

The Natural World This is pretty self-explanatory. It’s been a favorite category the last two years, so we thought we’d bring it back for a third.

Harvest Show us the bounty of your own garden, your CSA share, or nearby farm stand. And remember, “harvest” can be a noun or a verb.

The Urban Landscape
Normally the word landscape brings to mind images of plants — but we’re thinking more along the lines of “cityscape” — the bones of the city, the structures, the architecture. In broad panorama or in detail. You get the idea.

Miniature Gardens
Fairy gardens. Terrariums. Rock garden troughs. The to-scale plantings that miniature railroad cars travel through. It’s a small but mighty category, we think.
