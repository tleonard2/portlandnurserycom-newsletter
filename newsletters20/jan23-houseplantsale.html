Tillandsias can be found growing on the ground, on the branches and trunks of trees, and attached to rocky cliffs. The sole purpose of their root system is to attach to a surface to gain needed light exposure.
Some Tillandsia actually have little or no root system at all. They absorb all the nutrients and water needed through their leaves. As with any other plant, duplicating their natural environment as closely as possible is the key to growing them successfully in your home.
It is a misnomer that air plants do not need light and water. They require light and water like all plants. They obtain these and other elements in less traditional ways than plants growing in soil.
Read more about growing air plants on our Tillandsia feature page.
A long-lived perennial plant, asparagus makes delicious spears every spring with only a little maintenance; but it takes 2-3 years to come into full production. Asparagus is commonly grown from 2 year old crowns (roots), which may reach bearing size somewhat faster than seeds.
Caring for Asparagus: Asparagus yields best in full sun (at least six hours of direct sun), but tolerates light shade. An area sheltered from strong winds is preferred. Provide well-drained soil with a neutral pH. Plant asparagus roots in spring as soon as the soil is dry enough to dig.
