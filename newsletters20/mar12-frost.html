March 12, 2020
We're expecting one of those dips in temperature this weekend and we want you to be prepared! We have frost blankets and tunnel row covers that will do the trick on your newly planted veggies and other tender plantings. Even a light frost is enough to ruin sensitive flowers and vegetable crops.
Kale and chard that have been recently planted need to be covered and watered which will help insulate them. Peas, onions, garlic can be left uncovered and established kale and chard are fine uncovered. Lettuce and other greens, broccoli, cabbages should be harvested or protected with row covers, frost blankets or cold frames. Warm the soil with mulch made from yard debris, cardboard or newspaper.
Move containers up close the house, preferably in a spot that is protected from cold winds. Make sure your geraniums, fuchsias and other tender plants are tucked away.
Wrap your containers with frost blankets and burlap for insulation. Avoid using plastic to cover plants since moisture and air circulation is important. Adding straw or leaves under frost blankets will increase protection.
