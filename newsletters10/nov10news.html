Buy Ugly!

Great deals to be had at both locations!

We have so much great plant material in our clearance area right now. It will be fine next year, it just doesn't look like much now. Fall is the best time for planting because the winters are
mild enough temperature wise, the rains keep things moist and plants can continue to grow roots most of the winter.

If you are planting cold hardy (that means plants hardy to USDA zones 7 and below) deciduous plants will be perfectly fine even if we have a hard winter and so will most cold hardy evergreen plants. What you need to watch out for is the "borderline hardy" (plants rated to zone 8 or above) and things that need excellent drainage. This is because these plants will not have a root system established enough to withstand extremely cold temperatures and tolerate the copious amounts of rain we receive in the winter.

These are just basic guidelines for planting in the ground in unprotected areas. There are so many exceptions to the rules (of course it's gardening). In summary, what I would plant now:

Hardy deciduous and evergreen grasses, perennials, ferns, shrubs trees and
conifers. Ground covers that are not picky about soil drainage such as
Kinnickinick, Rubus pentalous, Pachysandra, and Vinca. Bulbs and garlic!
Ornamental cabbage and kale, pansies and dusty miller.

Things I wouldn't plant now in the ground but would be fine in a sheltered location such as a covered porch include hardy gardenias, large leaved Hebes, Phormiums, and plants that need good drainage such as creeping thyme. -- Laura

Plant Picks of the Month

TREE/SHRUB PICK - Callicarpa - Beautybush
If when strolling through the neighborhood this fall a sparkly purple thing catches your eye, chances are it is Callicarpa, commonly known as Beautyberry. Easy to grow, great for housing and feeding birds in winter, excellent for cutting to add to fall floral displays, Callicarpa is a must-have plant in the northwest. Read more...

NATIVE PICK - Physocarpus - Pacific Ninebark
If you have a naturally-moist area of your garden with space for a largish shrub to settle in and make itself at home, the Pacific Ninebark (Physocarpus capitatus) might be the perfect choice. In the wild it can be found along stream banks, at the edge of moist woodlands and wetlands; it will indeed languish if allowed to get too dry... Read more...

PERENNIAL PICK - Thymus
There are 350 species of thyme. Historically, thyme has been associated with courage, strength, happiness, and well-being. Most are frost hardy, small, aromatic evergreen perennials that flower late spring to mid summer. Creeping or mat forming types generally are used as groundcovers, while the small shrub forms are used for culinary purposes. Read more...
