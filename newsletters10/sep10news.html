In the Nursery

FALL IS IN THE AIR!

The nights are cool, school is back in session and the mums are blooming. Its time to get the last rounds of lettuce, carrots and beets, plant kale and collards for winter harvest. Broccoli, cauliflower and cabbage can be planted for a spring crop. Soon our garlic, shallot and onion bulbs will arrive (watch for the posting on Facebook arrival announcement). The bulbs can be planted into early November for next year’s summer harvest. This year we had a challenging time acquiring garlic due to crop failures as a result of the cool wet spring. We will still have an exciting selection, but fewer organic varieties. Softneck varieties include Italian Late, Early Italian Late, Silver Rose and Nootka Rose, organic Inchelium Red. Hardneck varieties include Spanish Roja, German red and organic Musik.

Speaking about summer harvest this has been the best year for green tomatoes! Did you know you can pickle green tomatoes? They are also great roasted and fried. Please post your favorite green tomato recipe on our Facebook page! With the spring and summer being so cool the tomatoes are just taking forever to ripen! To ripen tomatoes faster try pruning off any remaining flowers, new tips, shoots, and tiny green tomatoes. Also stop watering tomatoes that are in the ground or in very large (wine barrel size) containers. Some farmers stop watering their tomatoes when they set fruit to intensify the flavor and encourage them to ripen. A third tip is to root prune your plants with a shovel. This will shock the plants into ripening fruit quickly. Green tomatoes will eventually ripen if they are harvested green and spread out on a flat surface, or wrapped in newspaper and lightly layered in a paper bag. Many of us do this every October to get the most out of our tomato production.

Along with vegetables we are filled with mums, peppers and Rudbeckia (Black-eyed Susan) for bright fall color. Our Stark store also has a fabulous crop of perennial dahlias in #2 pots that are only $10.99! This is also a great time to come in and put together containers that will look great all fall and winter. We have a great selection of evergreen perennials, ferns, grasses and shrubs in 4” and gallon pots.

Also conifer season has begun! We have received a nice selection of specialty conifers from Iseli Nursery perfect for adding color and shape to fall planters or for adding texture and form to the landscape.

Check your faucets and hoses

FALL CHECKUP FOR YOUR HOSES AND FAUCETS

Tips from the Regional Water Providers Consortium

A 1/16th inch hole can waste 25,000 gallons a month

When was the last time you checked your hoses and outdoor faucets for leaks? As summer comes to a close it’s a good time to make sure your irrigation system is in good shape before you stow your hoses and sprinklers for the winter. Here are some tips from the Regional Water Providers Consortium:

1. Check your irrigation system and faucets for leaks. A hole just 1/16th of an inch in diameter in a hose or irrigation line can waste 25,000 gallons of water a month. A faucet that is dripping at the rate of 90 drops a minute can waste 288 gallons a month. The Consortium’s website has information and how-to videos on finding and fixing leaks.

2. Consider the type of irrigation you use to water different parts of your landscape and garden. For example, soaker hoses are best for beds that are densely planted. Where plants are spaced apart, an irrigation system that uses individual emitters for each plant is the better choice – or, water by hand.

3. If you have an in-ground sprinkler system, check to make sure the sprinkler heads water your plants and lawn and not the sidewalk or street. That’s water down the drain!

The Regional Water Providers Consortium is a group of over 20 area water providers and Metro that works to educate the public about water-related issues and the importance of conserving water. Learn more at www.conserveh2o.org.
