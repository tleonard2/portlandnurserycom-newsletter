Raymond Evison Clematis are HERE!

Using Thrillers, Spillers and Fillers to build containers!

This year marks 25 years of breeding and growing for Raymond Evison's clematis nursery. These plants are trialed for 8-9 years before they are commercially marketed to ensure quality and disease resistance. These clematis come in a whole new range of forms and sizes, with many having extended length of flowering season. Many are also suitable for patio containers. We have Asao, Bijou, Viennetta and many more. (Pictured below, left to right - Empress, Crystal Fountain, Rebecca.)

Come in and see these new varieties at both our Stark St. and Division St. locations.

 

New Perennial and Annual Varieties Featured!

Call and have your favorite new variety held 24 hours just for you!

Every year we are enchanted by new plants that come out. Just like you we get tempted by the photos in magazines and we just have to try new varieties in our display beds and home gardens. Here are some of our favorite new introductions that have come out with in the last few years. We think you can find a spot in your garden too!

Some annuals that have caught our eye include the Monroe series of Argyranthemum, with contrasting double centers in shades of rose and white. Pair these Marguerite daisies and Pennisetum ‘Fireworks’ (the new rose and red purple fountain grass) and Petunia ‘Pretty Much Picasso’ (a new trailing petunia with fuchsia flowers edged in lime green) and you will have an eye popping container!

In shade annuals the new Sweet Potato vine ‘Illusion’ comes in emerald and midnight. They are more compact than other varieties and have an exquisite lacy leaf that adds great texture. Try grouping these with the new Euphorbia ‘Breathless Blush’ and trailing alyssum look-a-like Lobularia ‘Snow Princess’ for an elegant shade hanging basket.
Also in our annual section (of our Stark location only) we are proud to announce a whole line of unusual treasures from Annie’s Annuals. We are the only nursery in Oregon to have their plants! Here are a few tempting photos to start the treasure hunt…(Pictured below, left to right - Gilia capitata “Blue Thimble Flower,” Omphalodes linifolia “Venus’s Navelwort,” and Psoralea pinnata “Kool-Aid Bush.”)

In sun perennials we are excited about the myriad of new Coreopsis. We trialed ‘Sienna Sunset’ in our parking strip last year and loved the apricot to orange flowers from April through September! Also starting to arrive are the New Millenium Delphiniums. These are tall English Delphiniums that have been selected to be long lived, heat tolerant and cold hardy. Erysimum ‘Fragrant Star’ is a charming new wallflower with variegated green and yellow foliage topped by soft yellow fragrant flowers. It only reaches 12-15” tall and is great edging boarders and as an evergreen component in containers.

In our ornamental grasses department, winner of the New Grass award at the 2009 Far West Show, is Variegated Northern Sea Oats, Chasmanthium ‘River Mist’ It sports crisp white and green striped leaves topped by white seed heads. Later in the season we look forward to the return of the Restios to our sales tables. They are a unique group of rush-like plants that add a very architectural look to containers.

There are so many new beauties in our shade perennials. The knock out newbies include Dicentra ‘Burning Hearts’ (pictured here) with lacy blue foliage and dark pink and white blooms, and Heucherella ‘Sweet Tea’ with deeply lobed bronze foliage. Ligularia ‘Osiris Fantaisie' and ‘Osiris Café Noir’ offer fabulous color and texture to the moist shade garden with their serrated burgundy leaves followed by golden daisy flowers. (Pictured below, left to right - Dicentra 'Burning Hearts,' Ligularia and Santolina 'Lemon Fizz.')

Santolina ‘Lemon Fizz' is perhaps the cutest best smelling introduction we have seen in years. It has bright chartreuse, stringy foliage that appears more like a thread leaf cypress than a lavender cotton. It will reach 12” tall and is hardy to Zone 7! Another attractive herb introduction has bee the variegated basil “Pesto Perpetuo.” One of our employees grew it in a container and brought it indoors in the fall and had fresh basil all winter!

Last but not least, we have many new exciting vegetables. We are happy to see the return of Sweet Potato starts! Log House Plants is offering a dozen varieties this year. Since sweet potatoes love heat, cover the soil with plastic, or grow them in a large container in an extra hot spot. Also through Log House are the amazing and bizarre “Believe it or not” vegetables. You can grow oddities such as the orange, spiney African Jelly Melon and brown ‘Poona Kheera’ cucumbers.

A cute, new tomato on the market is ‘Tomatoberry.’ This red tomato is the size and shape of a strawberry and matures in 65 days.

Since new varieties can be in short supply, and go quickly please call to see if we have a certain new beauty in stock. We will be happy to hold it for 24 hours for you.
