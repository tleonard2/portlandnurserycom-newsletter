AppleTasting at the Nursery

23rd Annual Apple Tasting Event

Wow. We thought the event couldn't get any bigger, but we swear you all came to this year's Apple Tasting, and from the looks of it you brought your friends along!

Rain or shine, you came; you tasted (and bought) apples; danced to a lively array of local music under the cover of our newest big tent; visited the cooking demos and vendors, sampled delicious hard cider.

Adults, kids, dogs (some of them in costume) – a great time was had by all – and money was raised for Elders in Action, canned food gathered for Impact Northwest. Thanks to one and all for helping make this an outstanding community event and a Portland tradition!

Plant Picks of the Month

TREE/SHRUB PICK - Physocarpus - Ninebark
Eastern Ninebark Physocarpus opulifolius and Western Ninebark Physocarpus capitata are the most popular types of Ninebark in nurseries today. Many cultivated varieties of Eastern Ninebark have emerged in the past ten years offering a selection of leaf-color and mature sizes that are very garden worthy. Read more...

NATIVE PICK - Physocarpus - Pacific Ninebark
If you have a naturally-moist area of your garden with space for a largish shrub to settle in and make itself at home, the Pacific Ninebark (Physocarpus capitatus) might be the perfect choice. In the wild it can be found along stream banks, at the edge of moist woodlands and wetlands; it will indeed languish if allowed to get too dry... Read more...

PERENNIAL PICK - Chrysanthemum
Chrysanthemums are showstoppers of the autumn garden. Each flower consists of ray florets in a rainbow of colors including white, yellow, green, bronze, orange, red, pink, purple, and countless bicolors. They are classified into a dozen groups according to flower form. The aromatic foliage, oval to lance-shaped and 2" to 7" in length, may be shallowly or deeply lobed or even feathery... Read more...
