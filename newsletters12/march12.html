First day of Spring is March 20!!!

Spring officially starts March 20th this year, and the season is already off to an exciting start. There are many new varieties to anticipate for 2012, here is a sneak peak! For the collectors shade garden we will have five varieties of lady slipper orchids (Cypripedium sp.) arriving in early May and ranging in shades of pink, gold and red.

Also for the shade will be Leucosceptrum japonicum 'Golden Angel' which looks like a chartreuse coleus but it is perennial! This is an introduction from Dan Hinckley and will be available in limited numbers in early May.

There are so many fantastic introductions in sun loving perennials. Some early blooming varieties include, Iberis 'Absolutely Amethyst', truly something different for a candy tuft with large clusters of lavender flowers in April and May, atop evergreen foliage.

There are many new wallflowers this year: Erysimum 'Winter Sorbet Improved' provides stunning contrast with fragrant, rust orange flowers which then fade to lilac; Erysimum 'Winter Rogue' has gorgeous rose red flowers fading to purple. Scabiosa 'Mariposa Violet' is a fantastic new pincushion flower with large, fully double flowers on long stems that are great cut flowers. This variety blooms through October, longer than older varieties and resistant to powdery mildew!

A is for April and Artichokes (and a new special tomato)

Starting in late April we will have three varieties of the Jerusalem Artichoke edible tuber, easy to grow perennial vegetables:

  • Stampede is the classic type with rounded, knobby chokes and a heavy yield.
  • Fuseau has long tubers which are easier to prepare in the kitchen, and the plants are slightly taller than Stampede.
  • Red Fuseau has long magenta tubers that have a sweeter, snappier flavor!

INDIGO ROSE TOMATO

Also in our vegetable section we will have starts for the Indigo Rose tomato which has already gotten a lot of attention. The skin turns dark purple when exposed to full sun while the flesh remains red. It is high in anthocyanins and was bred at OSU. Read more about this unique tomato.

 

Annuals bursting through

The world of annual plants is bursting with too many exciting new varieties to mention them all here. We saw the new Callibrichoa 'Cherry Star' briefly last year and instantly fell in love with the uniquely patterned pink and yellow flowers. 3D Osteospermum offer a new flower form to the choices of African daisies with a double center that give them a fluffier look. A lovely new Petunia for baskets this year is 'White Russian'. It has white flowers with black veins that would look great with Petunia 'Phantom' and Ipomea 'Blackie' for a black and white combination. An irrestiable, durable and exotic group of coleus is the 'Under the Sea' collection. They really look like shrimps and corals, and they combine great with Juncus 'Afro'.

Plant Picks of the Month

PERENNIAL PICK:
Dicentra - Bleeding Hearts are a long standing, popular staple in the shade garden. Old-fashioned bleeding heart, Dicentra spectabalis, has been cultivated in gardens since the 1840's and still reigns as the most widely grown of all the Dicentra. Read more..

TREE PICK:
Corylus avellana 'Contorta' Contorted Filbert, Harry Lauder's Walking Stick
These are some of our favorite plants for winter interest because the branches twist and turn in such a beautiful way. Read more...

NATIVE PICK:
Goodyera oblongifolia: Western Rattlesnake Plantain. There is one species of wild orchid that has been making its way more frequently into availability through careful propagation: Goodyera oblongifolia, or western rattlesnake plantain. Read more...

March's Magnificence: Designed by Katie Gwynn at Portland Nursery using these plants:

1. Clematis x cartmanii 'Avalanche'
2. Iberis 'Purity'
3. Trifolium 'Dark Dancer'
4. Bellis perennis
5. Liriope muscari 'Pee Dee Gold'
