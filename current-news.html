June 11, 2020
Save some space for round two!
It is so exciting when spring vegetable starts begin to fill the shelves! Beets, Brussels sprouts, cabbage, lettuce, Swiss chard, peas, seed potatoes and shallots are just to name a few.
With all these great crops it is easy to fill up your whole garden in one day. However, we want to remind you to save some space for replanting fast maturing crops (such as lettuce) for a continual harvest. This practice is commonly referred to as "succession planting", and will help you maximize the harvest season by continually planting new areas of varieties that will ripen sequentially.
To continue reading our article on succession planting including suggested planting times and veggie combinations, see our Succession Planting Article.
More Information
You also might enjoy reading this Margaret Roach article in the New York Times on Succession Planting. We will have a variety of starts so you can succession plant as Margaret mentions: cilantro, beans, and chard through July. All of our Fall and Winter starts will have harvest labels around the 3rd week of July.
You may have noticed it has been difficult to acquire specific varieties of vegetables - especially if it is organic - because the demand for them have sky rocketed since the start of COVID-19. In addition to this, we are getting to the natural time of year when growers are in between crops or sold out.
Our growers have done an amazing job at shifting their production towards growing as many vegetables as possible. This has led to a shortage in other non-edible crops and some popular annuals such as marigolds and impatiens.
Our buyers are working hard to bring in all of the plants that are available. While we may not have the exact variety of plant you have in mind, we are happy to help you find a stellar substitute. Finding solace in our gardens during these unprecedented times is more important than ever, so let’s get creative and do our best to have fun!!
Thank you for your amazing support, patience and understanding.
