June 11, 2020
Save some space for round two!
It is so exciting when spring vegetable starts begin to fill the shelves! Beets, Brussels sprouts, cabbage, lettuce, Swiss chard, peas, seed potatoes and shallots are just to name a few.
With all these great crops it is easy to fill up your whole garden in one day. However, we want to remind you to save some space for replanting fast maturing crops (such as lettuce) for a continual harvest. This practice is commonly referred to as "succession planting", and will help you maximize the harvest season by continually planting new areas of varieties that will ripen sequentially.
To continue reading our article on succession planting including suggested planting times and veggie combinations, see our Succession Planting Article.
More Information
You all might enjoy reading this Margaret Roach article in the New York Times on Succession Planting.
We’ll a variety of starts so you can succession plant as Margaret mentions: Cilantro, beans, and peas through July. We will have Fall & Winter starts labeled with harvest labels around the 3rd week of July.
We are getting to that natural time of year when growers are in between crops or sold out. You may have noticed it has been difficult to acquire a specific tomato or pepper variety (especially if it is organic). Recently we did not have any marigolds.
When we became aware of the presence of Covid 19 in March, everyone was scrambling to get veggies. There were several weekends in which we had nothing on the shelves in March. Our growers did an amazing job at shifting production towards growing veggies while they were short staffed with an unprecedented demand. They were able to do this by not growing as many annuals such as marigolds and impatiens.
The demand for organic veggies and herbs is so much higher than the supply. We have been rationed only two flats per variety in some cases this whole season. Finding an organic Shishito pepper is like hitting the jack pot. We understand that our bodies are out temples and eating only organic is a mantra for some. Some of our conventional growers such as Langley and Log House use organic methods but are not certified. None of our growers use GMO seed (GMOs are actually not available at the retail level).
Now on to other plants. Since Oregon is one of the nation’s top nursery growing area, some of our growers ship all over the country. Other growers that stay a little closer to home. All of our growers have seen small accounts triple the amount they usually order because the demand for plants around the country is huge. For the first time ever our growers have run out of all crops much faster than planned for back in the summer of 2019. Our buyers are doing the best they can to bring beautiful plants for your gardens. While we may not have the exact plant you are looking for, we may be able to find a substitute. As we have heard these are unprecedented times, so let’s get a little creative and do our best to have fun.
Thank you for your amazing support, patience and understanding.
