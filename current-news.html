January 16, 2020
Blueberries make the perfect addition to any garden, because they are easy to grow, and especially fun for households with children. They come in many different sizes, and can be grown in pots allowing them to fit into even the smallest of gardens.
Blueberries prefer full sun—at least 6-8 hours per day in the spring and summer. They do best in well-drained soil with an acidic pH of 4.5 to 5.5. Soil in Portland is generally slightly acidic already, but fertilizing with an organic acid plant food (such as for azaleas) will help maintain the right pH. For blueberries in pots, use a planting mix formulated for acid-loving plants.
When summer comes, blueberries will need lots of water, one inch per week during dry months, and two inches per week while flowering. To retain moisture add two inches of mulch around the roots of the plants (be sure not to pile mulch around the stems, it just goes on the roots!) and reapply once each year.
Most blueberries are self-fertile and will produce fruit when only one variety is planted, but planting at least one more variety will boost production and berries will be larger.
Harvest season can be extended through summer and into fall by planting blueberries that ripen at different times. Check this Blueberry Ripening Schedule for details.
Blueberries are not only easy to grow, but decorative as well. The flowers are dainty and bell-shaped with unique pink and blue coloring. In the fall after you’ve enjoyed all those tasty berries, the leaves turn beautiful shades of red, yellow and orange, giving you vibrant fall color. Their stems add color and form to barren winter gardens. Blueberries have much more to offer than tasty berries, making them the perfect plant for year-round interest.
