Iris versicolor
Japanese Iris (Iris ensata) have large flattened blooms in late spring to early summer. Flower color varies from shades of purple and blue to pink and white. Japanese Iris have a distinctive yellow signal on the lower petals.
Some varieties have exquisite contrasting veining. Most Japanese Iris grow about 3' tall. They prefer moist soil and do not tolerate drought. They do well in shallow water on the edges of ponds or in rain gardens.
