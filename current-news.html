September 12, 2019
It's a great time to plant conifers.
On those infrequent sunny days, be ready with a conifer to plant! They love getting in the ground this time of year, settling in, and enjoying the fall rains. And we're bursting with stock this time of year - all your favorites and new specialty varieties you'll love.
Conifers serve faithfully as the 'bones' of the garden, providing year-round structure and backdrop to fruits and flowers. They can also be the star of the show with so many wonderful silhouettes, colors and textures available. Use conifers to frame an entrance, hide the neighbors, spruce up your fall and winter containers or decorate for the holidays.
Merriam-Webster describes Conifer as any of an order of mostly evergreen trees and shrubs having usually needle-shape or scale-like leaves and including forms (as pines) with true cones and others (as yews) with arillate fruit.
Our Conifer Selection page is a great place to start if you need some help picking the right tree. You'll find links to selecting a standard or miniature conifer along with tree selection guides, planting and pruning guides.
Sections include:
  • Classification and Rate of Growth, along with size in 10 years.
  • Shape definitions
  • Pruning tips
  • Selection tips
  • Selection Guides listing categories: evergreen and deciduous, hedges, shade conifers, containers sized conifers.
Visit our Conifer Feature Page!
In addition, we have many specific conifer pages.
Spruce trees are wonderful! They may be a tad prickly on the outside, but Piceas are colorful and reliable. They come in all shapes and sizes too, offering a wide range of garden-worthy options. See our Picea: Spruce feature.
Our native Firs (Abies) are given great consideration about once a year when they make nice holiday trees, but our native forest conifers are so much more and indeed many are worth considering for the landscape. See our Native Abies feature and also our Standard Abies feature.
Cryptomeria is the national tree of Japan. It can be found planted at many sacred sites and is also planted on a large scale as a forest tree. Only one species exists, Cryptomeria japonica. It grows fast into a very large lofty, pyramidal or conical evergreen tree, with red-brown bark which peels in vertical strips. The wood is scented, waterproof, lightweight and strong, so it is used for all types of construction in Japan. See our Cryptomeria feature for more information.
Freshen up your winter containers with miniature conifers. They come in a wide array of colors and textures but grow so slowly that they can thrive for years in a pot. Some are tidy little buns, others are cones so small they may as well be gnome hats and a few have quirky irregular shapes that grab your attention. See our Miniature Conifers feature for selection and growth rates.
