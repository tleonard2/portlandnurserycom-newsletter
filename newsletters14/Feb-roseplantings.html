Treating our Roses and Fruits with love
Our newsletter yesterday mentioned availability
of "bare-root roses and fruits" in error.

Both roses and fruits come to us bare-root, but we have found that potting them up for you guarantees that we'll deliver healthy plants.

Bare-root plants arrive at Portland Nursery in January and February from reputable growers in Oregon and California. Upon arrival they are treated with mycorrhizal fungi, a beneficial fungus that helps roots to grow faster, and are then potted to allow for root production and protection.

All are sold in pots, rather than bare-root, which protects their roots from breaking and drying out. You can find our best selection of roses and fruits in March and early April when our potting process is complete.

2014 Rose lists

Each year, we offer more than 300 different varieties of roses. They begin to arrive in February and our selection is complete early in March. All our 2014 variety lists are on our website now to help you in planning your rose garden.

Rose flowers are hard to resist, so when they bloom, our selection fades. Please call in advance to either of our locations to check current availability.
