Seeds Are Here!
There is truly an air of excitement when the full seed racks go up in the store – for staff and customers alike -- our palms practically itch with the anticipation of the coming spring and this year’s garden possibilities! We have spent the short, snowy days of winter with our seed catalogs and computers, and now that spring is just around the corner and we’re gearing up for another season of growing!
This year, we have managed to squeeze in a wide selection of seeds including organic and conventional, heirloom, and new and improved hybrids. In addition to seeds, we carry all of the supplies needed to successfully grow them indoors, including sterile seed starting mixes, fiber pellets, coir pots, trays, humidity domes, seedling heat mats, plant markers, soil thermometers, garden inoculant and small indoor grow-light systems!
Make your lists, mark your calendars and come visit Portland Nursery to find the perfect seeds for your garden. Some seeds need to be started under grow-lights a full eight weeks before average last frost, which here in Portland means you’ll be revving up your seed-starting operation mid-February which will be here before you know it!
If you don’t know what varieties to grow we recommend heirloom varieties. Most of them originated before the 1950s and have been preserved and passed through multiple generations by seed saving. These were chosen because they “grow-true” and carry a remarkable trait, such as high yield, unique flavor, color or shape, exceptional disease resistance, heat tolerance, adaptability to soil types, etc. If you want to learn more about heirloom vegetables check out our brochure. Basic Instructions for Seeding Indoors
1) Read instructions on back of selected packet to find the spacing, light and moisture requirements, as well as how long it will take for crop to grow.
2) Prepare tray or cell packs by filling with seedling soil mix. Place tray in standing water and let sit until the surface of the soil is moist. Take the container out of the water and allow it to drain thoroughly. Sow seeds thinly on to the surface by tapping them onto soil from a folded paper.
3) Lightly cover seeds with a fine layer of soil or vermiculite.
4) Place tray or cell pack in a sunny window or under a sun light. Cover with a piece of glass, plastic or plastic wrap over the top to maintain moisture. If necessary, cover with netting or newspaper to shade it from direct sun. When germination starts, remove both covers.
5) Continue to regularly water seedlings according to the back of the packet. When seedlings are large enough to handle, it’s time to either thin out the starts, or transplant them. If they were grown in cell packs, thin out by removing all but the strongest 1-3 starts in each cell. If grown in a tray, gently lift each seedling out of the soil - keeping as much of the roots intact as possible – and transplant each start into its own cell. Cover roots with soil.
6) Water in seedlings and use a waterproof stick tag and sharpie to label the plant's name, date of seed sowing and date of transplant.
