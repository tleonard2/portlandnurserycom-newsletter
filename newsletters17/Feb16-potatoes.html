Seed potatoes have arrived!
Unlike the potatoes from your local grocery store, our seed potatoes are not sprayed with any sprouting inhibitor, which means they will produce more shoots, roots, and have a higher yield!
Time to plant your potatoes is still a couple weeks away, but now is the time to come in and get the varieties you'd like before they sell out! Portland’s prime potato planting season starts in mid-March and goes through the end of May with the ideal soil temperature of 55° to 70°F.
1. If possible, select tubers the size of an egg and plant them whole. Larger tubers should be cut up into pieces weighing about 2 ounces and containing two or more eyes. Use a clean, sharp knife to cut seed pieces the day before planting. Once cut, they must be planted the next day.
2. Plant seed potatoes 4-6” deep, 12” apart, in rows 12-24” apart. If the soil is heavy, plant 2” deep and cover with 2” of mulch. A good yield requires regular watering and full sun.
3. Sprouts will emerge about 2 weeks after planting which is when you should begin fertilizing, using a balanced fertilizer such as EB Stone Tomato & Vegetable.
4. When the stems grow to about 8” high, it is time to start hilling. Gently hill up soil around the vines until only 4” are exposed using soil from both sides of the row, compost, or any other light but moisture retentive material. Repeat the hilling process every time the vines reach 8” tall until you are out of soil to hill up or space to make hills.
5. Once harvest time comes near and yellowing of the plant begins stop fertilizing and watering, it is time to allow soil to dry out.
6. After the vines die down leave them for about 2 weeks, this allows time for the skins to “set”, which increases the potatoes storage ability. They keep best in the dark at 36° to 40°F, with good air circulation and just enough humidity they don’t dry out.

Our bare root roses arrive in February from reputable growers in Oregon and California. Although roses come to us bare-root, we have found that potting them up for you guarantees that we'll deliver healthy plants. Upon arrival they are treated with mycorrhizal fungi, a beneficial fungus that helps roots to grow faster, and are then potted to allow for root production and protection.
