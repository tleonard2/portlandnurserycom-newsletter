What is a gesneriad? The gesneriad (pronounced either “guess-NARE-ee-ad” or “jez-NARE-ee-ad”) family contains over 3,200 species of plants. The best-known member of the gesneriad family is the African violet (Saintpaulia).
In addition to the African violet, some of the more common gesneriads grown by hobbyists are the florist gloxinia (Sinningia speciosa), lipstick plant (Aeschynanthus), goldfish plant (Nematanthus), cape primrose (Streptocarpus), flame violet (Episcia), and Cupid’s Bower (Achimenes).
Many gesneriads bloom throughout the year, an especially welcome sight on a cold winter day. This is a plant family of great diversity, and many grow under the same conditions we enjoy. Many of the easiest and most beautiful plants for the home or greenhouse belong to the gesneriad family. Some gesneriads go dormant for a short period of time. These are either tuberous or rhizomatous gesneriads.
Most gesneriads are well-suited to life in the home. Normal home temperatures are usually acceptable for gesneriads. Daytime temperatures of 65° to 75°F, with a 10° drop at night, are considered ideal for most gesneriads. Some require higher temperatures, others will accept much lower ones. Avoid hot or cold drafts, such as heating or cooling vents or open windows or doors. Overall, if you are comfortable, your plants will be also. Read more about growing Gesneriads on the Gesneriad Culture website.
