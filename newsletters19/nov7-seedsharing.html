Tree wrap is used to prevent Sun scald which can damage newly planted and thin-skinned trees like; fast growing maples, beech, honeylocust and crabapples. On cold winter days, the sun can heat up bark to the point where the vascular system is stimulated.
Then when the sun sets and temperatures drop rapidly, the active tissue is easily destroyed. Sun scald can be prevented by wrapping the trunk with a commercial tree wrap or any other light-colored material. Trees planted on the south sides of buildings can be especially vulnerable and most of the damage will show up on the southwest sides of the tree.
Apply the wrap early in November. Start at the bottom of the trunk, overlapping the wrap as you wind it upward. Secure the end with a staple or small tack. Don't use tape or twine, as it may girdle the tree. In April, remove the wrap so insects or diseases aren't tempted to move in.
An easy way to remember... wrap by Thanksgiving and unwrap by Easter. Newly planted trees should be wrapped for at least two winters and thin-barked species up to five winters or more.
