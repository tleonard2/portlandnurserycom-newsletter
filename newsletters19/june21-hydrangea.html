What's a Northwest Garden without a Hydrangea? Hydrangeas make a wonderful addition not only to your garden but also do well as a patio or deck plant. Their hardiness keeps them blooming year after year. Potted up, they become a colorful addition to your outdoor living space and care is easy. Get a summer bloomer like "Endless Summer" and you'll have gorgeous blooms all summer long!
Most Hydrangeas perform best when planted in dappled or afternoon shade. Those in full sun often burn and require extra watering. Pee Gee Hydrangeas (H. paniculata), and Oak Leaf Hydrangeas (H. quercifolia) are exceptions that can be planted in sunny spots but will still perform well in part shade. Soil should be consistently moist and well-drained.
The spent blooms of hydrangea make for winter interest. In the spring, cut off the spent blooms, prune out a few stems of old wood and watch it fill out and bloom again!
Repeat Blooming Hydrangeas
The latest Hydrangea breeding has produced a dizzying array of repeat flowering plants. Old-fashioned Hydrangeas set buds on the previous year’s growth, but the new Hydrangeas set flower buds on both old wood and new wood. In Portland, this means flowers from June through frost!
