March 14, 2019
In our newsletter today, we are featuring Clean Water Grow® products made just down the road in Hillsboro, Oregon. Clean Water Services, a water resources management utility committed to protecting Oregon’s Tualatin River Watershed, produces a line of plant food blends that are perfectly formulated for your garden. From flowers and shrubs to fruits and vegetables, each blend provides plants with a season-long supply of vital nutrients.
By choosing Clean Water Grow you are supporting sustainable recovery of essential plant nutrients, helping to preserve sensitive local waterways and advancing research and development of innovative water management solutions. Part of their proceeds support the Clean Water Institute™, a non-profit committed to scientific research, environmental education and the protection of water resources.
What makes Clean Water Grow® unique?
Your garden will love you.
Clean Water Grow® contains Crystal Green®, a Root-Activated™ nutrient technology that supplies sustainable, slow-release phosphorus, nitrogen and magnesium in one compound. It delivers a season-long supply of nutrients that release as your plants grow.
Plants need minerals like phosphorus to thrive. The used water that flows through resource recovery facilities is full of this vital nutrient. Clean Water Grow® harvests this nutrient in the form of a pure phosphorus prill and add it to each blend to keep your plants happy.
It's stream friendly.
Clean Water Grow® is slow release. Nutrients are released only when plants need it, reducing the chance of fertilizer leaching into the soil and washing away into rivers and streams.
Clean Water Grow® is one-and-done.
One application can last the entire growing season, leaving you with more time to enjoy your garden.
A blend for every part of your garden.
Four unique blends include All-Purpose, Tomato & Veggie, Rose & Flower and Lawn.
"Grow is my go to fertilizer, giving my plants the food they need when they need it.  I mixed it in my planters and the plants went insane!"-Deb Paltrow, Oregon Gardener
