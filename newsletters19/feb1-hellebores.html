Our favorite part about Hellebores is that they bloom when almost everything else is just trying to make it through winter, including us. They're guaranteed to brighten up those overcast Portland days and remind us that spring is just around the corner. The genus of Helleborus is comprised of 15 different species and belongs to the buttercup family (Ranunculaceae). Read more...
Hellebores, are perennial and largely evergreen and the perfect solution for year-round interest in a shade garden. Their flowers provide color and interest when other plants are dormant. In addition, their leaf structure is interesting enough to make them a foundation of the shade garden, even when their flowers are spent. Hellebores are also deer resistant and somewhat drought tolerant.

The weather in February is usually still quite harsh, and despite the frequent rainfall, spells of dry freezing air are still possible.
There is a lot of fresh, new plant material coming in throughout the month, so get ready to liven up those porch containers and, if your soil is workable, your garden beds as well. Add primroses around a walk, plant some seeds indoors, get some structure in the garden with miniature evergreens, add cyclamen indoors and out.
It's also a good time to get your small fruits in the ground: strawberries, blueberries, currants and many more. Our 2019 buyer's lists - Berries & Small Fruit and Fruit & Nut Trees - have both been published and are available here on our website Fruit Page.
Missed bulb planing? We have potted crocus, hyacinths, and daffodils for delicate color spot additions. Note that it is still too early for starting most of these outside, but you can store the bulbs for a few weeks until fairer spring weather.
