DON'T HESITATE - VEGETATE!
May is a fine time for starting carrots. We often do not have starts for these and some other root vegetables because it is often better to grow them from seed. This is also true of other members of the carrot family as well as radishes and, to some degree, beets. When growing these root crops ensure consistent and moderate soil moisture and use soils that have a somewhat high organic matter content and no rocks or pumice.

Tomatoes are at their Peak!

Tomatoes are the quintessential for the summer veggie garden for many people. Everyone’s passions come out when the tomatoes come in. It’s no wonder they were once called “love apples.”

Our tomato selection is ramping up here at Portland Nursery! We will be carrying over 300 varieties this year with our peak selection between the last week of May through mid to late May.

Planting tips

  • Tomatoes need at least six hours of direct sun.
  • Amend soil with compost or manure, and use an all-purpose fertilizer (such as Sure Start) and a handful of lime in the bottom of the hole.
  • Use Cozy Coats, Season Starters or a cold frame to protect your tomatoes when night temperatures are between 46º-55º to avoid stunting.
  • Red plastic tomato mulch can help warm the soil when the weather is cool, and retain moisture when it is hot.
