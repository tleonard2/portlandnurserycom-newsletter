Fall Planting Season is Here!

Autumn is the very best time for planting. Soil temperatures remain warm for a significant period of time after the air grows cold encouraging root growth, while the cool air reduces water loss through leaves and bark. In addition, because the moisture of the soil in fall is generally high, the plant can weather a warm day.

As with planting at any other time of the year, the key is to keep the roots from drying out.

  • Water your plants before planting to ensure that roots don’t dry out.
  • Dig a hole as deep as the root ball and at least twice as wide.
  • Fill the hole with water and let it drain. If it takes forever for the water to drain, add compost to the soil that was taken from the hole (1 part compost, 3 parts soil) and blend it well.
  • Place the plant in the hole making sure the crown of the plant is at the same level or slightly higher than the level at which it was grown and us soil mixture to fill the hole. Deeper planting can cause rotting.
  • Water well. Set a soaker hose or sprinkler at a trickle and leave it on for 30 minutes, then grab a trowel and prod the soil to make sure the soil is wet.
  • Add 1-2” of mulch to help keep moisture in. Make sure mulch covers the root zone and is not piled around the base of the plant like a volcano.
Treebate

Every tree planted in Portland contributes to clean rivers and healthy watersheds. Plant an eligible tree on a residential property between September 1, 2015 and April 30, 2016 and Environmental Services will credit your city water/sewer utility bill for half the purchase price.

How to Get Your Treebate

  • Purchase a tree between September 1, 2015 and April 30, 2016.
  • Plant it in your Portland residential yard. Complete and submit a Treebate form by April 30, 2016-include the tree purchase receipt with the purchase price circled.
  • Receive a credit on your city water/sewer utility bill.
