Your deck will thank you!
With gorgeous blooms and lush green year after year

Hydrangeas make a wonderful patio or deck plant. Their hardiness keeps them blooming year after year. Potted up, they become a colorful addition to your outdoor living space and care is easy. Get a summer bloomer like "Endless Summer" and you'll have gorgeous blooms all summer long!

Their spent blooms make for winter interest. In the spring, cut off the spent blooms, prune out a few stems of old wood and watch it fill out and bloom again!
Looking to change up the colors?
Plant a Bigleaf Hydrangea (Hydrangea macrophylla) and you can change the blue and pink flowers by changing the soil pH.

  • Low pH is acidic = blue flowers
  • Higher pH is more alkaline = pink flowers
  • Mid-range pH = purple flowers

Soils west of the Cascades are typically acidic, so Hydrangeas are typically blue when planted in the ground.

To Change Colors:

  • To turn flowers purple or pink, add lime to the planting area & reapply twice a year.
  • To deepen blue color, add Aluminum sulfate once a year.
  • To maintain a specific pH, test the pH twice a year using a soil test kit.
  • White hydrangea color cannot be manipulated with soil pH.

pH Flower Color

  • 4.5 deep, intense blue
  • 5.0 medium blue
  • 5.5 lavender-purple
  • 6.0 pink-purple
  • 6.5 mauve-pink
  • 6.8 medium pink
  • 7.0 intense, deep pink
