Pixie Grapes

Pixie Grape is a dwarf grapevine that produces miniature clusters of grapes. This small plant is easy to care for and can be grown both indoors and out. In fact, the Pixie Grape is so small, it can even be grown in a coffee cup!

Pixie Grape is a naturally occurring mutant of Vitus Vinifera ‘Pinot Meunier’, a variety of grape used to produce wine and champagne. Unlike traditional vines that can take up to three years to produce grapes, the Pixie Grape begins producing its signature miniature clusters of grapes within the first year of growth. Due to its unique appearance, it makes a wonderful conversation piece and is a must-have for the wine lover!

Portland Nursery is proud to support Sauvie Island Center!

We were delighted to be a sponsor of the Sauvie Island Center Annual Summer Barn Dance & Picnic. A warm evening, great food, music, dancing, beautiful scenery, fun auction items, and donation incentives from yours truly all made for a very memorable summer event. All the celebration was for a really worthy cause: this year marks the 10th anniversary of Sauvie Island Center’s wonderful farm education programs!

Established in 2005, the Sauvie Island Center teaches youth about food, farming, and the land. In fun and educational field trips and camps at Sauvie Island Organics farm and Howell Territorial Park, students get to visit and explore a real, working farm, pioneer orchard, and nature park. Hands-on activities allow kids to learn about soil, botany, pollination, wildlife, and the food web. Students get seed-to-harvest growing experience in the Grow Lunch Garden, where they plant, tend, harvest and eat fresh vegetables from their own field.

We think there’s no better way to give kids a lasting connection to nature and to their food! To find out more about Sauvie Island Center, and how you can help support their programs (and even get a gift card from Portland Nursery in the process!), visit the Sauvie Center Website!
