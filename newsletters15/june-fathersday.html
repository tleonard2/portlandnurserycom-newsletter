Father's Day!
Make it a day in the garden with the kids

Gardening with children can be one of the most rewarding experiences for both the child and their parents or mentors. Children are natural gardeners - they are curious, learn by doing, and love to play in the dirt. Working together in the garden can provide hours of quality time each week and encourages a child’s interest in the workings of nature and science, experiencing the cycles of life first hand.

We've gathered a few articles we think might assist with making gardening interesting to kids! Start by downloading our brochure GARDENING WITH KIDS, then check-out the list below.
ATTRACTING BUTTERFLIES WITH NATIVES
Many butterflies are native to our region, and one way to attract them is by planting natives. Also, planting the larval plants that will feed the growing caterpillars will help to insure that you will have generations of returning butterflies to your garden. Visit this page for attracting (and keeping) butterflies using natives. Additionally, read about Milkweed, a food source for Monarchs!
BEE FRIENDLY PRACTICES
Bee-keeping can be a rewarding hobby, as well as a source of honey. Even if you don’t have the time or room to keep honey bees, you can support mason bees or other native pollinators. Contact the Oregon State Beekeepers Association at 503-657-5399. Download this brochure to find tips for how to best support this fragile resource.
ATTRACTING HUMMINGBIRDS WITH NATIVES
With a little planning, you can provide a native bloomer for these delightful birds throughout the season (of course, there are plenty of non-native plants that are hummingbird magnets, too, so you can do a lot of mixing and matching to suit your own garden and tastes). Visit this page for a year-round selection of hummingbird-attracting natives.
CREATING A BIRD HAVEN
Portlanders are lucky to live in a city with so much green space. Our combination of rivers, parks and mild climate allows Portland to host a vast array of birds and wildlife, and many will visit or even nest in local yards given a few simple provisions. Read how you can create a bird sanctuary right in your own yard!
BEWARE! POISONOUS PLANTS!
And it wouldn't be a complete list of gardening with kids without the What to Avoid Section! There are several poisonous berries in our area and we wanted to point out a few of the most common. This is a short list of plants that are potentially very dangerous to ingest and should be avoided.
