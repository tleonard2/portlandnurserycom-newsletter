We have all you need to install a seed lawn
It's not too early to get the ground ready for a new seed lawn. The optimal time to seed a lawn in the Pacific Northwest is between Sept. 1st and Oct. 15; but second best is between April 15th and June 15th.
  1. Calculate the square footage of space for your new lawn. To figure square footage, simply multiply the length and width. A 7-10 pound bag of seed will cover 1000 square feet.
  2. Examine the amount of light the area receives. There are seed mixes for every kind of situation. Shade, sun, high traffic, water conservation, and environmentally sound seeds are now available.
  3. Prepare your soil for the seed (or sod).
    • Roto-till when the soil is not too wet.
    • Add compost to help increase drainage (air and water flow) through the soil.
    • Add lime to raise the pH level, 5lbs. per 100 square feet
    • Use the roto-tiller to blend lime and compost

Creating a Pollinator-Friendly Landscape in Your Garden
Linn County Master Gardeners 4th Annual Pollinator Conference
WHEN: Saturday, March 3 -- 8 am to 5 pm PLACE: Albany Expo Center 3700 Knox Butte Dr.
For more on Mason Bees and other bees, take advantage of the Linn County Master Gardeners 4th Annual Pollinator Conference on Saturday, March 3 (speakers: 9:00am- 5:00pm). A full day of wonderful speakers, vendors, and citizens who are concerned about the plight of our pollinators!
Topics and Speakers include:
Flowering plants—bringing the pollen to pollinators
Dr. Linda Hardison and Dr. Dennis Albert of OregonFlora. Topics include: plant diversity, why plants matter, Oregon Flora resources and tools, designing your garden, and what works in the Willamette Valley.
Mason Bees-The Super Pollinators
Susan Morton and Ranee Webb, Linn Master Gardeners. Topics include: How to care for Mason Bees, what they do for us, their life cycle and the challenges they face.
Is Your Yard a “Green Desert"?
Rich Little, retired entomologist and Linn/Benton Master Gardener Topics include: Creating a sustainable environment in your yard for our native pollinators plus ways we may be making our yard an unfriendly place for bees. Learn about hardscaping techniques that help pollinators be more successful.
Vendors will have mason bee supplies (houses, tubes, blocks and cocoons), books, seeds, prints/photos, lavender products and more. Bring a small cooler and towel if you plan to buy mason bee cocoons.
Seating is limited to 200 people, so don’t delay in registering.
Visit the Linn Master Gardener website for details on the conference and for registration. We suggest that you order a lunch or bring a lunch. There are other events at the Expo Center that day, so leaving mid day to get a lunch is not a good option.
If you can not attend you can sign up for periodic informational e-mails (Bee Notes) at Linnmastergardener.com.
