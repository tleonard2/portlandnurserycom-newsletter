Backyard Birds
We live in an area that welcomes birds and wildlife all around us. Inside the city limits of Portland, we have 14,505 acres of park space - Forest Park itself is 5,188 acres! Our combination of rivers, parks and mild climate allows Portland to host a vast array of birds and wildlife.
Extend the green space by turning your own yard in to a Bird Haven. It's fairly simple and with a few steps you can attract birds by providing the three basic needs of Cover, Food and Water.
Start with Native Plants:
Incorporating as many native plants as possible provides for a bird’s two most basic needs, food and shelter.
  • Mahonia: Oregon Grape
  • Malus Fusca: Crabapple
  • Rhamnus purshiana: Cascara
  • Rubus: Salmonberry
  • Symphoricarpos: Snowberry
Link to our article on Bird Havens for tips and plant suggestions that provide cover, food and water for birds, the key elements to create a bird safe space in your own back yard!
And if you want more, visit our feature page on Natives that attract hummingbirds.
