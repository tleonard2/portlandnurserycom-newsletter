April 19, 2018
Earth Day is by now a standard tradition in most people’s lives. Having been around for nearly 50 years, there is an entire generation (or two) for whom the 22nd of April is as much a part of their annual calendar as any other holiday. People turn out in droves to mark the day to appreciate the natural world through acts of service and celebration.
Earth Day 2018 focuses on fundamentally changing human attitude and behavior about plastics and catalyzing a significant reduction in plastic pollution. From poisoning and injuring marine life to disrupting human hormones, from littering our beaches and landscapes to clogging our waste streams and landfills, the exponential growth of plastics is now threatening the survival of our planet.
For more information on the impact of plastics, download the Earth Day Network's Plastic Pollution Primer and Action Toolkit for Earth Day 2018. Visit the Earth Day Network website for additional ways to take action to End Plastic Pollution on Earth Day and beyond!
Gardening is an earth friendly activity!
As gardeners, we especially are aware of the need to take care of the environment that feeds us, body and soul. And really, every day can be Earth Day – the cumulative effect of small efforts can indeed be great, can indeed make a difference.
Here are some ideas to try for your own celebrating of Earth Day:
  • Start composting your yard debris – it makes a rich and wonderful soil amendment that you can cycle back into your garden – you can build a composting system or purchase a pre-made bin of wire or recycled plastic. We have a variety of compost bins to select from to help any household decrease their output of waste.
  • Even though we Portlanders can now also recycle our kitchen scraps, it's still fun to have a worm bin – makes a great family project, too – it's a sort of garden nerdy thing, but amazing to watch bits of vegetables, fruit peels and egg shells turn into rich material for your soil (or as a great addition to your compost pile). You can purchase a pre-made worm bin or make your own.
  • Disconnect your downspout and install a rain barrel - your garden will thank you and you'll save some money on your utility bills.
  • Grow some of your own organic food. It's better for you, better for the earth. You don’t need a lot of ground, either; lots of vegetables and herbs grow happily in containers, and some even in shade.
  • If you have the available space, why not plant a tree? Trees attract birds to your garden; provide shade that helps to cool your house and cut down on need for air-conditioning; and fruit trees have the added benefits of attracting pollinators to your yard and give you tasty fresh produce.
  • Make room for native plants in your landscape. Natives need less supplemental water, attract native butterflies and other important pollinators, provide food and shelter for birds.
